Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 6, 2026
Last reviewed: May 6, 2026

Free Calorie Track is designed to respect your privacy. The app does not require an account, does not sell personal data, and stores your nutrition data locally on your device.

1. Information We Collect

Free Calorie Track does not collect personal information such as your name, email address, or physical address.

All food logs, calorie entries, macro targets, and history data are stored locally on your device using browser storage (localStorage, sessionStorage, and IndexedDB). This data does not leave your device unless you choose to export or delete it.

Your nutrition data remains in your browser's local storage indefinitely until you manually delete it or clear your browser data. The app does not automatically delete old entries.

Third-Party Food Database APIs

When you search for foods or scan barcodes, the app queries the following external nutrition databases:

  • USDA FoodData Central – A public database operated by the U.S. Department of Agriculture containing nutrition data for branded foods. USDA data is in the public domain (CC0 1.0 Universal). When you search for branded foods or restaurant items, your search queries and IP address may be logged according to the USDA Privacy Policy. The USDA API is rate-limited to 1,000 requests per hour per IP address.
  • Open Food Facts – An open-source nutrition database operated by a non-profit organization. When you scan barcodes, your barcode numbers and IP address may be logged according to their privacy policy.

Free Calorie Track does not share any personal information with these services. Only your search queries, barcode numbers, and IP address (automatically sent with all web requests) are transmitted. No user accounts, food logs, or other personal data leaves your device.

AI Food Logging (Optional Feature)

When you use the AI food logging feature, the food description you type or the photo you take is sent to Google Gemini via our server (hosted on Vercel) to estimate nutrition content. This is an optional feature that you must actively choose to use each time.

What is sent to Google Gemini:

  • The text description of your meal, or
  • A compressed image of your food (resized to 800px maximum dimension, approximately 70% quality)

What is not sent: your profile, food logs, account information, or any other personal data. Our server acts as a relay only — it does not log or store the image or description after the request completes. The nutrition result is returned to your device and stored locally.

Google's handling of API requests is governed by the Google Privacy Policy and Google Terms of Service.

Nutrition Coach (Optional Feature)

When you use the Nutrition Coach feature and tap "Analyze my week," the following data is sent to Google Gemini via our server to generate a personalized weekly analysis:

  • Your food logs from the last 7 days (calorie totals and food names)
  • Your calorie and macro targets (protein, carbs, fat)
  • Your profile data: fitness goal, activity level, age, and weight

The app presents a disclosure screen listing exactly what will be sent before you confirm. This feature is opt-in and requires your explicit confirmation each time you use it for the first time, with an option to review the disclosure at any time thereafter.

Our server does not store this data after the request completes. The coaching result is returned to your device and saved only in your local storage. Google's handling of this data is governed by the Google Privacy Policy.

2. Analytics

Free Calorie Track uses Vercel Analytics to understand general usage patterns, such as page views and feature usage. This data is aggregated and does not identify individual users.

Vercel Analytics does not use advertising cookies and does not track users across websites.

3. Camera Access

Two features may access your device's camera:

  • Barcode scanner: Camera access is used only when you activate the scanner. Barcodes are read in real-time on your device. No images or video are stored or transmitted beyond the barcode number sent to Open Food Facts (described above).
  • AI Food Logging: If you choose to photograph your meal using the AI logging feature, the resulting image is compressed and sent to Google Gemini as described in Section 1. Camera access for this feature is only used when you explicitly choose to take a photo, and only that photo is transmitted.

Camera access is never used passively or in the background.

4. Cookies and Local Storage

The app uses local storage and other technical browser features strictly necessary for the app to function (for example, saving your logs and preferences).

It does not use cookies for advertising, profiling, or cross-site tracking.

5. Payments, Donations, and Affiliate Links

Free Calorie Track is supported through:

  • Optional donations via Buy Me a Coffee
  • Affiliate links (such as Amazon Associates)

Free Calorie Track does not process payments directly and does not have access to your payment information. If you choose to support the app or click an affiliate link, the transaction occurs entirely on third-party websites. These third parties have their own privacy policies governing how your data is handled.

6. Hosting and Infrastructure

The app is hosted on Vercel. The domain is registered through Cloudflare. These providers may process limited technical information (such as IP addresses) as part of normal website delivery and security operations.

International Data Transfers: Vercel operates globally and may process data in the United States and other countries. By using Free Calorie Track, you consent to this processing.

7. Your Rights and Choices

You have the right to:

  • Access your data – Use the Export feature in Settings to download all your data in JSON format
  • Delete your data – Clear your data via browser settings or the app's Settings page
  • Export your data – Download your data for portability to other apps
  • Opt out of analytics – Use browser settings, ad blockers, or Do Not Track settings

To exercise these rights, use the Settings page within the app or contact us at FreeCalorieTrack@gmail.com.

8. California and U.S. State Privacy Rights

If you are a California resident, you have specific rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), including the right to know what personal information is collected, request deletion, and opt-out of "sales" of personal information.

We do not sell personal information. The app stores data locally on your device and does not share it with third parties except as described in this policy (USDA FoodData Central API queries, Open Food Facts API queries, Vercel Analytics, and Google Gemini when you use the optional AI Food Logging or Nutrition Coach features).

For more information or to exercise your rights, contact us at FreeCalorieTrack@gmail.com.

9. European Economic Area (EEA) and UK Users

If you are located in the European Economic Area or United Kingdom, you have certain rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and UK data protection laws.

Data Controller: Free Calorie Track does not operate as a traditional data controller because all personal data (food logs, weight entries, profile information) is stored locally on your device, not on our servers. However, to the extent GDPR applies, Unexpectable LLC is the data controller for any analytics data collected via Vercel Analytics.

Your Rights Under GDPR:

  • Right of Access: Access your data by exporting it via the Settings page
  • Right to Rectification: Edit or correct your data within the app at any time
  • Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): Delete your data via the app's Settings page or browser storage settings
  • Right to Data Portability: Export your data in JSON format via the Export feature
  • Right to Object: You may object to analytics processing by using browser privacy tools or ad blockers
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: You may stop using the app at any time and delete your data

Legal Basis for Processing: We process data on the following legal bases:

  • Local storage of nutrition data: Consent and Legitimate Interest (necessary for the app to function)
  • Analytics data: Legitimate Interest (improving app performance and user experience)
  • AI Food Logging and Nutrition Coach: Consent (you actively initiate each use; Coach requires explicit opt-in confirmation before data is sent)

Data Transfers: The app is hosted on Vercel, which may process data in the United States and other countries outside the EEA/UK. Vercel complies with applicable data transfer mechanisms, including Standard Contractual Clauses.

Complaints: If you believe your data protection rights have been violated, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority (supervisory authority).

For questions about your GDPR rights or to exercise them, contact us at FreeCalorieTrack@gmail.com.

10. Children's Privacy

Free Calorie Track is intended for adults and users aged 13 and older. While the app is not designed specifically for children, we do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children under 13.

If we learn that we have inadvertently collected information from a child under 13, we will delete it promptly.
