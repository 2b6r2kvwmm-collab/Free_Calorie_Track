## What is Free Calorie Track?

**Perfect for:**
- Weight loss and fat loss
- Muscle building and bulking
- Macro tracking (protein, carbs, fat)
- Maintaining a healthy weight
- Athletes and fitness enthusiasts
- Anyone looking to understand their nutrition

## Core Features

### 1. Barcode Scanner

**What it does:** Scan any packaged food to instantly log nutrition information.

**Why it matters:** Instead of manually searching and typing, just point your camera at the barcode. It's fast, accurate, and eliminates the hassle of data entry.

**How it works:**
1. Tap the barcode icon in the food logger
2. Point your camera at the product barcode
3. App finds the product and auto-fills nutrition info
4. Adjust the serving size if needed
5. Add to your log

**Works with:** Thousands of packaged foods, including:
- Grocery items
- Snacks and beverages
- Protein powders and supplements
- Pre-packaged meals
- International products

### 2. Comprehensive Food Database

**What it includes:**
- Thousands of common foods (fruits, vegetables, meats, grains, packaged items)
- User-added packaged foods via barcode scanner
- Accurate USDA nutrition data for whole foods
- Macro breakdowns for every item

**Search functionality:**
- Type any food name (e.g., "chicken breast," "banana," "brown rice")
- Filter by meal type
- View recently logged foods for quick access
- Save favorite foods

### 3. Macro Tracking

**What you track:**
- **Calories** - Total energy intake
- **Protein** - Essential for muscle preservation and satiety
- **Carbs** - Energy and performance
- **Fat** - Hormone production and satiety

**Visual progress bars** show you how much of each macro you've consumed vs. your daily goal. Learn more about [tracking your macros](/macro-tracker) with real-time progress.

The Macro card also includes a **goal overview** with your target macro split visualized as a donut chart, so you can see at a glance whether your protein, carbs, and fat are aligned with your goals.

**Why macros matter:**
- Calories determine weight change (gain/loss/maintenance)
- Protein helps preserve muscle during fat loss
- Hitting macro targets improves body composition
- Balancing macros affects hunger and energy

**Flexible goal setting:**
- Customize your own calorie and macro targets
- Or choose from research-informed presets based on your goals (fat loss, muscle gain, maintenance)
- App tracks progress throughout the day
- Adjust goals anytime as your needs change

### 4. Additional Nutrition Tracking

**What it tracks (optional):**
- **Fiber** - Digestive health and satiety (goal: 25–35g/day)
- **Sodium** - Heart health and blood pressure monitoring
- **Sugar** - Total sugar awareness for metabolic health
- **Saturated Fat** - Cardiovascular health tracking

**How to enable:** Settings → Nutrition → turn on Additional Nutrition Tracking.

Once enabled, fiber, sodium, sugar, and saturated fat appear in your food log entries alongside calories and macros. A second page in the Macro card shows your progress toward daily targets.

**Who it's for:**
- Anyone managing heart health or blood pressure (sodium)
- People on GLP-1 medications who eat smaller volumes and need to maximize nutrient density
- Those tracking digestive health (fiber)
- Anyone wanting a more complete nutritional picture beyond just calories and macros

Targets are calculated automatically based on your calorie goal, or you can set custom values in Settings.

### 5. Daily Calorie and Macro Calculator

**What it does:** Calculate your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) and personalized macro targets.

**Inputs:**
- Age, sex, height, weight
- Activity level (sedentary to very active)
- Goal (lose fat, build muscle, maintain)

**Outputs:**
- TDEE (maintenance calories)
- Recommended calorie target for your goal
- Optimal protein, carb, and fat ranges

### 5. Meal Planning

**Organize food by meal type:**
- Breakfast
- Lunch
- Dinner
- Snacks

**Why it helps:**
- See macro distribution across meals
- Identify if you're front-loading or back-loading calories
- Plan ahead for the rest of the day
- Spot patterns in your eating habits

### 6. Custom Foods and Recipes

**Add custom foods:**
- Name your food (e.g., "Mom's Lasagna")
- Enter calories and macros
- Save for future use

**Powerful Recipe Builder:**
- Scan ingredient barcodes to add them instantly
- Or manually search and add each ingredient with weight/amount
- App automatically calculates total nutrition
- Divide into servings
- Save the entire recipe for one-tap logging in the future
- Perfect for meal prep - build once, use forever

**Great for:**
- Homemade meals you make regularly
- Batch-cooked meal prep
- Family recipes
- Restaurant dishes you replicate at home

### 7. Progress Tracking

**Weight logging:**
- Log weight daily or weekly
- View weight trend over time
- Track progress toward your goal

**Visual charts:**
- Weight loss/gain graph
- Calorie intake over time
- Weekly and monthly averages for calories and macros
- Macro adherence rate

**Full history access:**
- View and edit all past food and exercise logs
- Make corrections to previous entries
- Review your nutrition patterns over time

### 8. Favorite Foods

**Save your most-logged items:**
- One-tap access to foods you eat regularly
- No searching or scanning needed
- Perfect for meal prep staples

**Example favorites:**
- Morning coffee with creamer
- Your go-to protein shake
- Regular snacks (yogurt, fruit, nuts)

### 9. AI Food Logging (Beta)

Logging a meal just got faster. Tap "Log with AI," describe what you ate or snap a photo — of your plate, a restaurant dish, or even a recipe on a card or screen — and AI fills in the estimated calories and macros automatically. You review everything before it saves.

**How it works:**
- Describe your meal in text ("grilled salmon with roasted broccoli, about 6oz") or take a photo
- Google's Gemini AI analyzes it and returns estimated calories, protein, carbs, and fat
- Review and adjust the numbers before confirming
- Up to 10 AI-powered logs per day, completely free

**A few things worth knowing:**
- AI estimates are good but not perfect — adding context like portion size, cooking method, or brand improves accuracy
- Photos and descriptions are sent to Google for analysis under their privacy policy; nothing is stored by us
- You can see exactly what gets shared before you opt in
- Manual logging works exactly the same as always — AI is an option, not a requirement

### 10. Nutrition Coach (Beta)

Log at least 3 days in a week and a Nutrition Coach card appears in the Trends tab. Tap it once a day to generate a personalized analysis of your week — what you're doing well, where there's room to improve, and specific, concrete things to try.

**What it analyzes:**
- Your calorie and macro totals across the week
- How closely you hit your targets each day
- What foods you logged and your activity level
- Your goal (fat loss, muscle gain, maintenance)

**How it works:**
- Powered by Google's Gemini AI, run once per day
- Results are saved on your device — no account needed
- Completely optional; the rest of the app works identically without it

Coaching insights are estimates based on your logged data, not medical advice.

**Stay hydrated:**
- Set daily water goal
- Log water throughout the day
- Visual progress indicator

**Can be toggled on/off** in settings if you prefer to focus only on food.

## Tips for Success with Free Calorie Track

### 1. Be Consistent

**Track every day**—even weekends, even when you overeat. Consistency beats perfection. You can't fix what you don't measure.

### 2. Weigh Your Food

Invest in a $15 food scale. It's the difference between guessing and knowing your intake.

👉 [Learn how to use a food scale](/blog/how-to-use-a-food-scale)

### 3. Prioritize Protein

Protein keeps you full, preserves muscle, and has a high thermic effect. Aim for 0.7-1g per lb body weight. Use our [free protein tracker](/protein-tracker) to monitor your daily intake.

👉 [High protein low calorie foods](/blog/high-protein-low-calorie-foods)

### 4. Don't Stress Over Perfection

If you go over your calories one day, it's fine. Weight loss happens over weeks and months, not single days. Just get back on track tomorrow.

### 5. Use the Barcode Scanner

Scanning is faster and more accurate than searching. Use it for all packaged foods.

### 6. Create Favorite Foods

Save your regular meals (morning coffee, protein shake, go-to snacks) so you can log them in one tap.

### 7. Review Weekly Averages

Instead of stressing over daily fluctuations, look at your weekly average intake. This smooths out variability and shows true trends.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Free Calorie Track really 100% free?

**Yes.** Every feature is free. No premium tier exists. We don't charge for anything.

### Do I need to create an account?

**No.** You can start using the app immediately. All your data is stored locally on your device.

### Can I use it on my phone?

**Yes.** Free Calorie Track is a web app that works on any device with a browser (iPhone, Android, tablet, desktop). Just visit freecalorietrack.com.

### Does it work offline?

**Yes.** You can log food, view your database, and track meals without an internet connection.

### Is my data private?

**Yes.** All data is stored locally on your device. We don't collect, sell, or share your information.

### Can I track macros, not just calories?

**Yes.** Free Calorie Track shows protein, carbs, and fat for every food. Set custom macro goals and track progress in real-time.

### Can I track recipes?

**Yes.** Use the recipe builder to add all ingredients, calculate total nutrition, and divide into servings.

### How accurate is the calorie data?

We use USDA nutrition data for whole foods, which is highly accurate. For packaged foods, accuracy depends on the barcode database—but it's the same data other apps use.

### Can I export my data?

**Yes.** You can export your data via the settings menu in the app.
