## The Espresso: Pulls Fabulous Shots

**This machine pulls fabulous shots of espresso by default.** Once you have it dialed in, it's incredibly consistent.

### Dialing It In

New users will have to experiment with:
- **Grind size** - Finer for slower extraction, coarser for faster
- **How tightly to pack the grinds** - Tamping pressure affects extraction
- **Shot length** - You can customize to your exact preference

**It takes a little practice to dial in**, but once you've got it figured out, this machine is super consistent. I rarely have bad shots anymore.

The machine provides proper extraction pressure, and you can see real-time feedback on the front pressure gauge. This helps you learn what proper extraction looks like.

## The Single Boiler: Not a Big Deal

One thing to know about this machine is that **it's single boiler**, which means you can't pull shots and steam milk at the same time.

### Does This Matter?

**For most people: No.**

Even for me—someone who makes 4-6 drinks each day for the household—it's not a major issue. Here's why:

**Workflow:**
1. Pull espresso shot (~30 seconds)
2. Switch to steam mode (~10-15 second heat-up)
3. Steam milk (~90 seconds for 8 oz)
4. Total time per drink: ~2.5 minutes

**Dual boiler machines** (which let you steam and pull shots simultaneously) are either:
- **Vastly more expensive** if good quality ($1,500+)
- **Vastly inferior** if cheaper

For home use, single boiler is totally fine. You're not running a coffee shop—the slight wait between shots and steaming is negligible.

---

## Steaming Milk: Takes Time But Works Great

**Milk takes a little while to come up to temperature**—about 90 seconds to steam 8 oz of milk to ~145°F.

**Just don't expect hot milk in 10 seconds like at a coffee shop.** Commercial machines have way more power. But for home use, this machine steams milk perfectly fine.

Once the milk is hot, it creates excellent microfoam for latte art. The steam wand has enough power to get the milk texture you want.

## Things to Know Before Buying

### 1. It Takes Counter Space

The machine is large:
- **Dimensions:** ~13.5" W x 16" D x 16" H
- **Weight:** 23 lbs

Make sure you have the counter space for it. It's a permanent fixture on our counter.

### 2. There's a Learning Curve

If you've never made espresso before, **expect a week or two of experimentation** to dial it in. Your first few shots might be sour or bitter.

But once you figure it out, it's incredibly consistent.

### 3. It Requires Some Maintenance

**Daily:**
- Wipe the steam wand after each use
- Empty the drip tray

**Every 6 weeks or so:**
- Backflush with a cleaning tablet

**Every few months:**
- Descale the machine

It's not a lot of work—minimal maintenance for the quality coffee you get.
